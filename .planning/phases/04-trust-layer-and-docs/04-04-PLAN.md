   - "use client" directive.
   - Wraps in `<DocsLayout title="How It Works">`.
   - Content in casual gamer tone (short sentences, direct, game metaphors):
     a. **The Game**: Pixel Predict is a prediction game. An AI artist paints a 10x10 canvas, one pixel at a time. You bet on what color the AI will pick next.
     b. **The Loop**: Every 30 minutes, a new round opens for the next pixel. You pick one of 16 colors, place your bet in SOL, and wait. The AI makes its choice. If you called it right, you split the pot with other winners.
     c. **The Canvas**: 100 pixels, 100 rounds, one season. When the last pixel fills in, the season is complete. New canvas, new game.
     d. **The Odds**: This is parimutuel — no house edge on the odds. The payout depends on how much SOL is in the winning color's pool vs the total pot. A 5% rake funds the treasury and jackpot.
     e. Short paragraphs, no bullet lists. Conversational.

4. **The AI Artist page** (`app/src/app/ai-artist/page.tsx`):
   - "use client" directive.
   - Wraps in `<DocsLayout title="The AI Artist">`.
   - Content:
     a. **What the AI Sees**: The AI sees the entire canvas so far — every color it has already placed. It sees the neighboring pixels. It sees a summary of its last 5 choices. It sees a style direction for the season.
     b. **What the AI Doesn't See**: The AI never sees your bets. It doesn't know the pool sizes. It has no idea what color is popular. It can't be influenced by money.
     c. **How It Picks**: The AI is prompted at temperature 0 — fully deterministic. Given the same input, it always picks the same color. It chooses a color, a shade (how light or dark), and a warmth value (cooler or warmer hue shift).
     d. **The Commitment**: Before any bets open, the AI's input (the prompt) is hashed and posted on-chain. You can verify after the round that the prompt was locked before betting started. This is a prompt commitment proof — the game proves the AI's input was decided first.
     e. All copy uses "prompt commitment proof" language per user decision.

   - Content sections:
     a. **Bet Limits**: Minimum bet 0.01 SOL. Maximum bet per color per pixel: 10 SOL. You can increase your bet on the same color before lockout.
     b. **One Color Rule**: You can only bet on one color per pixel. Once you pick, you're locked in. No hedging.
     c. **The Clock**: Each round lasts 30 minutes. Bets lock 2 minutes before the round ends. Once locked, no new bets or increases.

     e. **Zero Winners**: If nobody picks the winning color, the net pool goes to the treasury. No refunds.
     f. **Claiming**: Winnings don't expire. Claim whenever you want from the My Bets drawer.

        - Look for the "Verified fair" badge

        - Click the Arweave link to see the full prompt text
